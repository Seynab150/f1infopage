Our top five Comedy books

At Freddie's by Penelope Fitzgerald, it is a tragicomedy that is set in a London stage school in the early 1960s that draws directly on her personal experiences.

Based on a True Story by Norm Macdonald, written by one of the greatest stand-up comics of all time, this book is a a manuscript about comedy, tragedy, love, loss, war, and redemption.

Bossypants by Tina Fey, it is a memoir which recounts episodes from her life along with her career as a comedian and writer for Saturday Night Live and 30 Rock.

Me Talk Pretty One Day by David Sedaris, it is a collection of twenty-eight short, hilarious essays, he writes about all his experiences and teaches us that knowledge can sometimes come from the unlikeliest of places.

The book that takes the number one spot is Everyone Hanging Out Without Me? (And Other Concerns) by Mindy Kaling, it includes witty confessions and unscientific observations as she writes about everything from being a timid young girl to living the Hollywood life.

Our personal monthly pick is That Moment When by Mo Gilligan, journeying through childhood memories in South London, Mo reminisces about school days and old-school raving, and takes us behind the scenes of his first comedy gigs all the way through to becoming one of TV's most in-demand stars.
