Our top five Non-fiction books

Sapiens by Yuval Noah Harari takes the number five spot, this book covers the whole of human history, how we first came to walk the earth to where we are now. This thought-provocing book allows us to challenge our perceptions and discover what makes us human and our future.

The Diary Of A Young Girl by Anne Frank takes the number four spot, this is a book detailing the day to day life of thirteen year old Jewish girl who fled to Amsterdam during the Nazi occupation. She, along with her family, lived in an annexe for two years before being discovered.

A Brief History Of Time by Stephen Hawking takes the number three spot, a book considered a landmark in scientific writing that explores what we know about the origins of the universe and how it works.

A Long Walk To Freedom by Nelson Mandela takes the number two spot, one of the greatest political leader of our time and Nobel Peace Prize winner. It follows his journey from his life as a young boy to his arrest during apartheid in his own country of South Africa.

The Immortal Life Of Henrietta Lacks by Rebecca Skloot takes the number one spot, it follows the story of a poor African-American tobacco farmer whos cells were taken without her ever knowing, and were vital in developing the polio vaccine, viruses, IVF and cancer research. Yet she was never acknowledged and her family has never profited from this multimillion-dollar industry.

Our personal monthly pick is Men Who Hate Women by Laura Bates, In this urgent and groundbreaking book, Laura Bates goes undercover to expose vast misogynist networks and communities. It's a deep dive into the worldwide extremism nobody talks about.
