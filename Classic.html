Our top five Classic books

The Handmaid's Tale by Margaret Atwood, it is a dystopian novel set in New England that follows a Christian fundamentalist regime in the former United States that arose as a response to a fertility crisis.

Little Women by Louisa May Alcott, it is an autobiographical account of her life and her three sisters. As their father is fighting in the American Civil War, the sisters are at home with their mother, a very outspoken women for her time.

A Tale of Two Cities by Charles Dickens, it follows the story of the French Doctor Manette during the French Revolution, his 18-year-long imprisonment in the Bastille in Paris and his release to live in London with his daughter Lucie, whom he had never met.

To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee, it follows the story of Scout Finch, her brother and their father during the Great Depression. Their father, Atticus, is a lawyer and their family is reasonably well off in comparison to the rest of society.

The book that takes the number one spot is Anna Karenina by Leo Tolstoy, it is a tragic story that follows the rise and fall of the love affair between Anna Karenina and Count Vronsky and the conflict between human desires and societal roles.

Our personal monthly pick is Wuthering Heights by Emily Bronte, as darkness falls, a man caught in a snowstorm is forced to shelter at the strange, grim house, Wuthering Heights. It is a place he will never forget and there he will come to learn the story of Cathy.
