Our top five Fantasy and Sci-fi books

The Martian by Andy Weir takes the number five spot, it follows the story of one astronaut as he fights to survive whilst stranded on Mars after his crew evacuate as they thought he was dead, Mark finds himself stranded and completely alone with no way to even signal Earth that he's alive.

The Blade-Runner by Alan E. Nourse takes the number four spot, a classic science fiction book about healthcare. A fast-paced futuristic novel set in a world where medical treatment is reserved for a set class of people. The story follows Billy Gimp who is determined to help those who have been left behind, no matter how dangerous the mission.

The Colour Of Magic by Terry Pratchett takes the number three spot, This fantasy story features popular characters such as Rincewind the wizzard and his Luggage Twoflower. A story with a great plot and a bizzare sense of humour.

Children Of Blood And Bone by Tomi Adeyemi takes the number two spot, Tomi Adeyemi’s debut novel is a gripping tale set in fictional Orïsha in Africa where magic has been outlawed. Follow the story along with the protagonist, Zélie, as she goes against the monarchy with the help of a rogue princess.

The book that takes the number one spot is The Lord Of The Rings by J. R. R. Tolkien, One of the most popular books of all time, this three part series set in the fictional Middle-earth follows various characters on a quest. A wonderful tale of friendship and bravery involving a wizard, a dwarf, an Elf, a human and four hobbits.

Our personal monthly pick is Iron Widow by Xiran Jay Zhao, when 18-year-old Zetian offers herself up as a concubine-pilot, her plan is to assassinate the ace male pilot responsible for her sister's death until she decides to take on the entire patriarchal military system.
