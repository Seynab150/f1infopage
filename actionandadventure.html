Our top five Action and Adventure books

Kidnapped by Robert Louis Stevenson takes the number five spot, it tells the story of David Balfour, a young man who is recently orphaned. As David has now turned seventeen years old, he has decided it is time to go and seek his fortune.

The Lightning Thief by Rick Riordan takes the number four spot, it follows the story of Percy Jackson as he undertakes a quest to journey across the United States to catch a thief who has stolen Zeus' master bolt.

The Da Vinci Code by Dan Brown takes the number three spot, it follows the story of Robert Langdon and Sophie Neveu after a murder in the Louvre Museum leads to their involvment in a battle between the Priory of Sion and Opus Dei.

Moby Dick by Herman Melville takes the number two spot, it tells the story of a voyage across the world on the whaling ship the Pequod and its captain, Ahab, as they pursues the great Sperm Whale; Moby Dick.

The book that takes the number one spot is Treasure Island by Robert Louis Stevenson, it tells the story of young boy called Jim, as he watched over an inn in an English seaside town. When a new guest arrives and at the inn and scares everyone with threats of violence.

Our personal monthly pick is The Martian by Andy Weir, it follows the story of one astronaut as he fights to survive whilst stranded on Mars after his crew evacuate as they thought he was dead, Mark finds himself stranded and completely alone with no way to even signal Earth that he's alive.
